Definitions


Participant - any participant in the EUvsVirus hackathon.


Team Lead - main responsible and point-of-contact of a team.


Team - A group between 3-7 participants working on a specific project to solve a challenge.


Domain Lead - Coordinating the Domain including all its stakeholders. Leading the Domain Boards, managing the relationship with the Challenge/Mentor/Team tracks.


Domain Board - A body consisting of recognized and successful people to be a patron/inspirator for the domain. We expect from these people to conduct a webinar, host a session track or share related resources.


Challenge Track - https://euvsvirus.org/challenges/ is divided into 6 tracks. These are like 6 parallel hackathons inside of the EUvsVirus Hackathon.


Challenge Lead - The person, who is accountable and responsible for the Challenge track. They closely work and coordinate with the Domain Lead, Challenge Board Members, Mentor Leads and Team Leads. They have an overall view on schedule, track of the teams, webinars, events, and communication channels related to a Challenge.


Challenge Board - A body consisting of experts in the fields related to a challenge. These people should have a very high skill to see the big picture in their domain, and at the same time be capable of advising Challenge Mentors on how to execute. We expect from these people to conduct a webinar, host a session track or share related resources


Mentor Coordinator - Overlooking all mentors and skill mentors in one challenge track, gathering and managing all the mentors, mentorship logistics, allocation of mentors to teams, the progress of mentorship, final reporting.


Mentor - The Team’s Coach throughout the hackathon process, responsible for guiding the development process of approximately 5 - max 10 teams.


Skill Mentor - A person with expertise in one or more fields, who is able to support a team with a specific problem or need. The skill Mentor is committed to investing time throughout the weekend to assist in the growth and development of others.


Team Coordinator - Navigating, and giving skill guidance for the teams. They are responsible for keeping people well-informed on the team forming process, hackathon process, and looking for problems to solve. They are your main point of contact for any questions regarding your hackathon experience.


HQ - the organisational unit synchronising all the tracks / main organisers.

Submissions


How-to Submit


I. Textual Pitch Submission on Devpost


Composed of 7 questions that you should prepare in advance before submitting to DevPost:

  • The problem your project solves
  • The solution you bring to the table
  • What you have done during the weekend
  • The solution’s impact to the crisis
  • The necessities in order to continue the project
  • The value of your solution(s) after the crisis
  • The URL to the prototype [Github, Website,...]

This needs to be submitted, alongside the video by 27th April 09:00 AM CEST (UTC+2).

Here's an example project page.

Here’s a video on how to submit on DevPost.


II. Video Pitch


For the video part, you have two strict requirements:

  • No longer than 2 minutes
  • Uploaded to YouTube as a public video (it can be “unlisted” if you want to)

Judging Criteria


Impact Potential

What is the impact to society if the ideas is implemented at scale? Does this offer something that hasn't been solved already? Id it has been solved, deas this offer something different? Can it be scaled to meed thousands or even millions of people?


Technical Complexity

Technical complexity does not necessarily mean code. This could be anything, hardware designs, specialised test kits or even a complex but clear business plan


Prototype Completion

Is there a ready technical prototype of the solution? If yes, does it work well? Does the prototype present well the idea of the solution? How fast can the prototype be turned into a ready to use product?


Business Plan

It is essential to consider the feasibility, economic and societal value, market knowledge and sustainability of the solution. What resources are needed to implement the solution?

After Submission


After the submission deadline, your submission will be evaluated by a panel of 5 jurors using the evaluation criteria mentioned above on a scale of 1-5 for each criteria, and the sum of all the scores will be your final submission score.
Your submission score will be evaluated with other submissions within your challenge. If your submission is in the top 10%, highest ranked of the challenge, then your submission will be presented in front of a panel of highly recognised, leading experts in the domain of the challenge you selected. Then this panel will hold another voting with the same criteria, to select the winning project and two runner ups.
Winners will be notified and announced on the 30th of April via email.
